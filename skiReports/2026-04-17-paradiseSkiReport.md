Most likely to trigger in very steep terrain, along the base of cliffs, and on convex rollovers.

### Practical Summary

Today is a go for backcountry skiing at Paradise with a few important considerations:

**Road Access:** The gate opens at 10 a.m., giving approximately 6 hours before the uphill closure at 4 p.m. Plan your tour to be heading down well before 4 p.m. Tire chains are mandatory for all vehicles — don't leave home without them.

**Avalanche Conditions:** Overall danger is Moderate (Level 2) across all elevation bands — the level at which the majority of avalanche accidents occur. Primary concerns are afternoon wet loose slides (triggered by warming) and older wind slabs in upper terrain. Start early to take advantage of a firm morning snowpack before afternoon heating. Choose slopes under 35 degrees, avoid convex rollovers and terrain traps. Be off steep slopes before temperatures rise significantly in the afternoon.

**Snow Quality:** With 1–2 feet of fresh storm snow on the volcanoes, coverage is much improved from recent weeks. Expect good snow quality in the morning (firm overnight crust transitioning to variable conditions) followed by heavier, wetter snow in the afternoon. Upper elevation bowls should offer the best skiing conditions.

**Recommendation:** An early start (arrive at gate opening at 10 a.m.), ski the upper bowls in the late morning, and be off the big terrain by 1–2 p.m. to avoid the worst afternoon wet snow and stay within the gate closure window. This is a classic spring skiing day at Paradise — plan it well and it will be outstanding.
