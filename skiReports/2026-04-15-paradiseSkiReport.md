**Problem #1: Storm Slab**
- By Wednesday morning, most of the storm snow will already be on the ground. Start the day questioning slides and slabs on all layers of the new snow.
- Expect slabs to be thicker and firmer in wind-loaded areas. These spots could produce avalanches even if you don't trigger them directly.
- If the sun comes out, conditions may change quickly — it's always hard to say if it will make things better or worse.

**Problem #2: Wet Loose**

### Forecast Discussion

Don't be surprised if you encounter a wide range of conditions on Wednesday. Things might be very different as you gain elevation and as the weather changes through the day. Coupled with less field data than normal, there is a much greater level of uncertainty. Give yourself several options to adjust to what you find and the potentially changing conditions.

The new snow will fall on a solid old surface. It is expected to bond well to the warmer, irregular crust, but that could vary by elevation and across the zone. Below the new snow, the upper snowpack is strong, thanks in large part to the solid 3/25 crust.

The new snow will certainly help travel conditions. It may also hide many of the nasty obstacles encountered previously, including patches of bare ground and rocks. Don't let the powder fool you — take it slow.
